BUKIDNON

The Highland Paradise In The Heart Of Mindanao

With over 20 Municipalities, 464 Barangays, Bukidnon is a landlocked province located in the center of Mindanao Island, in the southern part of the Philippines.
The province is particularly known as the "pineapple capital of the Philippines" and also referred to as the "Food Basket" of the region. Bukidnon is also known for its highland paradise, with a predominantly rolling grassland plateau with an average elevation of 915 meters. It has a refreshing cool and moist climate throughout the year.
Bukidnon is also labelled as the highland paradise in the heart of Mindanao. While it derived its name from the Visayan term “bukid” for “mountain”.
Bukidnon is relatively cool and moist throughout the year. The refreshing cool climate in the Bukidnon plateau is attributed to its fairly high altitude. Not only is Bukidnon endowed with natural attractions, the province’s terrain is characterized by deep ravines and dense forest mountains which protect the province from storms. Bukidnon is also typhoon-free.
Bukidnon is home to several mountain ranges, including Mt. Kimangkil, Pantaron, Mt. Tangkulan, Mt. Tago, Mt. Kalatungan Range Natural Park and Mt. Kitanglad Range Natural Park, which contain the remaining tropical rainforests of the province. These mountain ranges host important watersheds and host habitat for critically endangered, economically important and endemic fauna and flora, such as the Philippine Eagle and Rafflesia. The highest mountain in the ranges, Mt. Kitanglad, is the second highest peak in the country.
